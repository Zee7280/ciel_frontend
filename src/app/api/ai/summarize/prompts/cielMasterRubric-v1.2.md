# CIEL PK — MASTER EVALUATION RUBRIC
## Community Engagement Report — Complete Scoring & Recognition Framework

**Document Status:** Master Rubric v1.2
**Issued by:** CIEL PK — Pakistan's first Community Impact Education Lab
**Total Sections:** 10 | **Total Points:** 100 | **Master bonuses:** up to +5 (added individually to obtained) | **Badge Levels:** 7

---

## PART 0 — CORE EVALUATION PHILOSOPHY

### 0.1 Recognition First
Every report must be read as a generous reviewer trying to identify what the student genuinely contributed. Every attempted section receives recognition. A section with any substantive content should not receive a very low score merely because the writing is weak, the explanation is simple, or the documentation is modest. A **blank** section receives zero; a **weak but attempted** section receives at least its recognition floor; **quality, specificity, evidence, outcomes, and verification** lift the score upward from there.

### 0.2 Supportive Calibration
Scoring is calibrated so that an honest student who:
- meets required participation hours,
- has verified identity,
- completes real community activities,
- identifies beneficiaries,
- provides at least some evidence,
- and writes a basic reflection,

usually reaches at least **Level 3 (Emerging Community Contributor)**. A student should not fall into Level 1 or Level 2 unless there are serious missing sections, missing hours, missing identity verification, missing evidence, or major integrity concerns. **Level 3 is the normal recognition band for honest basic participation.** Level 4 is the normal band for meaningful developing impact. Level 5 is for strong, evidence-backed work. Level 6 is for exceptional depth, verification, and sustained quality. Level 7 is reserved for truly transformative, measurable, sustained, partner-verified impact.

### 0.3 Quality and Quantity Both Matter
Do not evaluate only by numbers. A project serving **10 beneficiaries** can still be strong if it shows depth, consistency, preparation, skill, and meaningful change. A project serving **100 beneficiaries** can score higher if the larger scale is real, verified, organized, and meaningful. **Small scale with strong quality must be credited. Large scale with weak evidence must be moderated. Large scale with strong evidence and real impact must be rewarded.**

### 0.4 Do Not Penalize Meaningful Small Projects
If a student worked with a small group but provided deep support — teaching, mentoring, counseling, training, feeding, awareness building, or skill development — give appropriate credit. *Example:* A student teaching 10 children over multiple sessions with learning material and visible improvement may receive a strong Section 4 score even though the beneficiary number is small.

The evaluator recognizes: depth of engagement, continuity, preparation, care, skill-based contribution, beneficiary interaction, visible improvement, and reflection.

### 0.5 Reward Real Scale
A group reaching 100 or more beneficiaries should receive higher credit if the work is organized and verified. However, do not automatically give high scores for large numbers. First check: Is the beneficiary count realistic? Is it supported by evidence? Is the student's individual role clear? Were the activities meaningful? Do outputs connect to outcomes? Was the work repeated or one-time? Did the partner confirm the scale?

### 0.6 Resource Mobilization Must Be Highly Appreciated
Resource mobilization is a **high-recognition section**. Students must be strongly appreciated when they contribute or arrange: cash, in-kind goods, books, stationery, food, clothes, equipment, transport, venue, teaching materials, medical or hygiene items, digital tools, social media support, design work, photography, video editing, training material, technical support, volunteer time, professional skills, human resources, institutional support, sponsor support, NGO support, community support. **Resource mobilization is not only money. Time, skills, networks, creativity, and digital support are also valuable resources.** Borderline cases in Resource Mobilization should be scored generously.

### 0.7 Partnerships Must Be Highly Appreciated
A student who brings even one genuine partner should be highly appreciated. Partners may include: NGO, school, welfare organization, university department, faculty supervisor, student society, local business, brand, sponsor, hospital, clinic, government office, community leader, religious or community institution, digital collaborator, volunteer network. **One genuine partner is a meaningful achievement.** More than one partner should receive higher credit if the roles are real and explained.

### 0.8 Evidence Must Be Checked Carefully
Evidence is not a checkbox — the evaluator must inspect and comment on it. Evidence increases confidence. Evidence should not artificially inflate a weak project. Weak evidence should not erase genuine effort. Missing evidence may affect badge readiness even if the CII score is reasonable.

### 0.9 Compare Student Against Their Own Work First
Before comparing with other students, examine the report's internal balance: strong activities but weak evidence, strong resources but weak outcomes, high beneficiary count but shallow engagement, small beneficiary count but deep engagement, good SDG intent but weak outcome measurement. Explain this balance in the final comments.

### 0.10 Comparative Commentary Comes After Independent Scoring
Score each report independently first. Use cohort comparison only to explain relative position (below cohort standard / foundational / emerging / average / good / strong / excellent / transformative). **Do not curve students down.**

## PART 1 — THE COMPOSITE IMPACT INDEX (CII)

The **Composite Impact Index** is a single **0–100** score. First sum the 10 section scores into an **obtained** base (Section 6 internal bonus is scored inside Section 6 only). Then evaluate **each** master-level bonus from Part 4 individually, sum them (max +5), add to obtained, subtract penalties, and clamp to 100.

```
Obtained_Score        = min( Σ Section_Scores , 100 )
Master_Bonus_Total    = min( Σ Individual_Master_Bonuses , 5 )   ← each Part 4 bonus calculated separately
Penalty_Total         = sum of red-flag penalties (max −5 unless critical)
CII                   = clamp( Obtained_Score + Master_Bonus_Total − Penalty_Total , 0 , 100 )

Where:
  Σ Section_Scores      = sum of all 10 final section scores
                          (Section 6 may score up to 18 internally, but Obtained_Score never exceeds 100)
  Individual_Master_Bonuses = Part 4 categories #1–#5, each awarded on its own merits

Final CII is rounded to one decimal.
Badge level boundaries are UNCHANGED (Level 7 = 92+, etc.).
Practical reach for excellent reports: 88–100.
```

> **Note on Section 6 special status:** Section 6 (Resource Mobilization) is the only section that may exceed its face weight via an internal bonus pool of up to +3. CIEL PK designed this exception because resource mobilization — whether financial, in-kind, skill-based, time-based, or creative — is the most consistently undervalued form of student contribution. See Section 6 detail in Part 7.

### 1.1 The Seven Badge Levels

| Level | CII Range | Badge Title | Certificate Appreciation Line |
|-------|-----------|-------------|-------------------------------|
| **Level 7** | **92 – 100+** | **Transformative Impact Contributor** | Recognized for creating measurable, sustained, and transformative community impact. |
| **Level 6** | **84 – 91.9** | **Distinguished Impact Contributor** | Recognized for demonstrating exceptional depth, evidence, and commitment to meaningful impact. |
| **Level 5** | **75 – 83.9** | **Strong Impact Contributor** | Recognized for delivering strong, evidence-backed contribution with clear community value. |
| **Level 4** | **67 – 74.9** | **Developing Impact Contributor** | Recognized for meaningful participation and a developing contribution toward verified community impact. |
| **Level 3** | **58 – 66.9** | **Emerging Community Contributor** | Recognized for taking active steps toward community engagement and meaningful social contribution. |
| **Level 2** | **48 – 57.9** | **Foundation Stage Contributor** | Recognized for beginning the journey of community contribution with foundational effort and verified participation. |
| **Level 1** | **0 – 47.9** | **Participation Acknowledgement** | Acknowledged for engagement with the CIEL PK community service journey; further completion encouraged to reach certification standard. |

**Boundary rule:** At a boundary, assign the higher level. CII 58.0 → Level 3. CII 75.0 → Level 5.

**Section 6 special status:** Section 6 carries an internal bonus pool of up to +3 inside its own section score (max 18). Master-Level bonuses (Part 4) are calculated **individually** and added only to `Obtained_Score`; they do not inflate section weights beyond the 100 obtained cap.

## PART 2 — UNIVERSAL SCORING MECHANICS

### 2.1 The Anchor System (high-level shorthand)

| Anchor | Label | % of Section Weight | Use When |
|--------|-------|---------------------|----------|
| **5** | Distinguished | 95% | All criteria fully met; specific, concrete evidence; precise language; exceeds expectations |
| **4** | Strong | 82% | All criteria met; one minor gap in specificity or precision |
| **3** | Developing | 68% | Most criteria met but with notable vagueness or missing detail in one area |
| **2** | Foundational | 55% | Half or fewer criteria met; significant gaps or partial misalignment |
| **1** | Minimal | 40% | Criteria largely unmet; off-topic, vague, or violates an explicit prohibition |
| **0** | Not Attempted | 0% | Blank; no substantive content |

**Formula:**
```
computed_score   = section_weight × anchor_percentage
section_score    = max(computed_score, recognition_floor)   if section is attempted
section_score    = 0                                         if blank or not attempted
```

> The word "Distinguished" as an anchor does NOT automatically mean the student earns Level 6. Section-level anchors and final badge levels are separate systems.

### 2.2 The Universal Band System (internal scoring detail)

For per-component scoring within sections, an **internal 0–100 band scale** is used. This provides finer granularity than the 6-level anchor system. Bands and anchors map directly:

| Band | Internal Range | Label | Aligns to Anchor |
|------|----------------|-------|------------------|
| **5** | 90 – 100 | **Exemplary** | Anchor 5 / Distinguished |
| **4** | 75 – 89 | **Proficient** | Anchor 4 / Strong |
| **3** | 60 – 74 | **Developing** | Anchor 3 |
| **2** | 40 – 59 | **Beginning** | Anchor 2 / Foundational |
| **1** | 0 – 39 | **Insufficient** | Anchor 1 / Minimal |

The internal score is then converted to fractional points within the component's point allocation: **points = (internal / 100) × component_max**.

### 2.3 Universal Automatic Deductions

Applied to internal 0–100 scores after band assignment. Cumulative but cannot drive a score below 0.

| Trigger | Deduction |
|---------|-----------|
| Word count below minimum | −15 internal |
| Word count above maximum | −10 internal |
| Describes activities where prohibited | −20 internal |
| Describes results/outcomes where prohibited | −20 internal |
| Mandatory dropdown / selection empty | Component score = 0 |
| Mandatory free-text field empty | Component score = 0 |

Per-section frameworks (Part 3) may add additional section-specific deductions.

## PART 3 — LENIENT CALIBRATION ADJUSTMENTS

These supportive lifts are applied AFTER section scores are calculated.

### 3.1 Honest Participation Lift
If the student has all of the following:
- verified identity (Section 1 Criterion A passes),
- meets required individual hours,
- has at least one real activity,
- has some evidence (Section 8 not bypassed),
- has completed at least 7 of 10 sections,

then the final CII should normally **not fall below 58** unless serious integrity issues exist. This protects honest students from demoralizing scores.

### 3.2 Meaningful Work Protection
If the student clearly completed meaningful community work but evidence is weak: score the activity, resource, partner, and reflection sections for the work described, but **set Badge Readiness to Conditional or Admin Review Required**. Do not reduce the CII excessively for weak evidence unless evidence contradicts the claim.

### 3.3 Evidence vs Badge Readiness Separation
- **CII** measures contribution quality.
- **Badge Readiness** measures whether the certificate can be issued safely.
- A student can have a moderate or good CII but still require admin review if evidence is incomplete.

### 3.4 Critical Integrity Exception
The supportive lift does NOT apply when any of these are true:
- individual verified hours below requirement,
- identity not verified,
- report is mostly blank,
- no evidence of participation,
- evidence contradicts the activity,
- report appears fabricated,
- serious HEC compliance failure.

In these cases: assign the accurate CII and set badge readiness to **Resubmission Required** or **Admin Review Required**.

## PART 4 — MASTER-LEVEL BONUSES (Maximum +5)

Bonuses are applied AFTER all section scores and lenient calibration. **Evaluate each master bonus category (Part 4) individually.** List every awarded bonus in the `bonuses` array with its amount. **Total master bonus pool is capped at +5 across all categories combined.** Add the capped bonus sum to `Obtained_Score` only — then clamp final CII to 100.

> **Layered bonuses (platform rule):** Section 6 internal bonus (up to +3) is included **inside** `Section_6_Score` and therefore inside `Obtained_Score`. Part 4 master bonuses (#1–#5) are **separate line items** — each calculated on its own, summed (max +5), then added to obtained. Final CII never exceeds 100.

| # | Bonus Category | Max | Awarded When |
|---|----------------|-----|--------------|
| **1** | **Resource Mobilization Bonus** | **+1.5** | Meaningful cash, goods, skills, digital support, volunteer coordination, OR sponsor support is documented in Section 6. This master-level bonus is in addition to the Section 6 internal bonus pool. Even if the student has earned all 3 internal bonus points in Section 6, this +1.5 still applies if criteria are met. |
| **2** | **Partnership Bonus** | **+1.0** | Verified partner involvement OR partner-supported continuation documented in Section 7 + 8. Single deeply-engaged partner counts. |
| **3** | **Evidence Strength Bonus** | **+1.0** | Strong file evidence: partner confirmation, receipts, attendance sheets, photos, videos — backing major claims across sections. |
| **4** | **Outcome Measurement Bonus** | **+1.0** | Baseline/endline measurements, beneficiary feedback instruments, OR partner-confirmed outcomes present in Section 5. |
| **5** | **Leadership Bonus** | **+0.5** | Clear evidence of team leadership, coordination, planning, OR execution by the reporting student. |

**Master Bonus 1 tiered scale (explicit):**

| Tier | Trigger | Master Bonus 1 Value |
|------|---------|----------------------|
| **Full (+1.5)** | Section 6 base ≥ 12 OR Section 6 final ≥ 14 AND ≥3 categories with credible narrative (evidence not required) |
| **Strong (+1.0)** | Section 6 base ≥ 10 OR final ≥ 12 AND ≥2 categories |
| **Moderate (+0.5)** | Section 6 base ≥ 8 (floor met) AND ≥1 category with meaningful resource |
| **None (0)** | Section 6 base below floor or blank |

**Cap rule:** Even if all five master categories are eligible at maximum, total master bonus is capped at +5.

## PART 5 — RED FLAGS & PENALTIES

Penalties exist in **two complementary tracks**, both feeding a single global penalty pool capped at **−5 across the whole report** (unless critical non-compliance, which can exceed −5 and triggers Resubmission).

- **Track A — Red Flags (5.1, 5.2):** Catalogued by severity tier; trigger Badge Readiness changes; primarily for integrity issues.
- **Track B — Section-Level Inconsistency Penalties (5.3):** Per-section deductions for incoherent or inconsistent reporting; typically small (−0.5 to −1 per trigger); capped per section.

Both tracks sum into a single `Penalty_Total`, which is capped at **−5 globally** (5.4).

### 5.1 Red Flag Penalty Tiers

| Tier | Score Impact | Badge Readiness Impact |
|------|--------------|------------------------|
| **Low** | No major score penalty; mention in comments | None |
| **Medium** | May reduce 1–2 CII points | May lower to Conditional |
| **High** | May reduce 2–3 CII points | Admin Review Required |
| **Critical** | May cap level if participation or identity invalid | Resubmission Required |

### 5.2 Red Flag Catalogue

**Identity & Participation flags:**
- identity_unverified (Critical)
- individual_hours_below_requirement (Critical)
- team_hours_confused_with_individual (High)
- unrealistic_daily_hours (Medium)
- duplicate_logs / identical_duration_logs (Medium)
- activity_dates_inconsistent (Medium)

**Activity & Outcome flags:**
- number_inflation — beneficiary count unsupported by outputs (Medium)
- padding_low_quality_activity (Medium)
- padding_low_quality_outcome (Medium)
- outputs_repeated_as_outcomes (Medium)
- orphan_outcome (Medium)
- baseline_endline_regression (Medium)
- confidence_overclaim — "Directly Measured" without instrument (High)
- number_inflation_unverified — 500%+ improvement, weak confidence (High)
- no_challenges_claim (Medium)

**Resource & Partnership flags:**
- resource_claims_unsupported (Medium)
- partner_named_but_not_verified (Medium)
- ghost_partner — listed with no contact/evidence (High)

**Evidence flags:**
- generic_AI_style_reflection (Low)
- description_as_reflection (Medium)
- self_inflation — all 5s on competency (Low)
- internal_duplicate — same evidence file for multiple claims (High)
- stock_or_web_image (Critical)
- fabricated_document (Critical)
- minor_consent_gap with public visibility (High)

**SDG & Coherence flags:**
- target_drift (Medium)
- forced_alignment / duplicate_sdg (Medium → −0.5 section deduction)
- discipline_mismatch (Medium)
- integration_depth_mismatch (Medium)
- coherence_failure — S5 references activities not in S4 (High)
- scope_mismatch (Medium)

**Sustainability flags:**
- mechanism_without_evidence (High)
- policy_overclaim (Critical)

**Universal flags:**
- sustainability_overclaimed (Medium)
- same_evidence_reused_across_reports (Critical)
- evidence_contradicts_claim (Critical)
- report_mostly_blank (Critical)

### 5.3 Section-Level Inconsistency Penalties (Track B)

Each section has its own catalogue of inconsistency triggers — small deductions for incoherent or inconsistent reporting. These accumulate within the section, then feed the global `Penalty_Total` pool (capped at −5 in 5.4).

**Design principles:**
- **Small per trigger:** Typically −0.5 or −1 per occurrence, never more than −1.5 for a single trigger.
- **Per-section cap:** Each section's inconsistency penalties cap at the value shown below, preventing any one section from consuming the whole penalty pool.
- **Recognition-first protection:** Inconsistency penalties NEVER push a section score below its Recognition Floor. They reduce CII via the global penalty pool, not via the section score itself.
- **Specific, not punitive:** Each trigger names a concrete, observable problem. Vague "this section is weak" does not earn a penalty — that's just scored low on the rubric.

### 5.4 Combined Penalty Formula & Global Cap

```
Penalty_Total = clamp(
                  Track_A_Red_Flags  +  Track_B_Section_Inconsistencies,
                  -5.0,
                  0
                )

EXCEPT: if any of the following CRITICAL NON-COMPLIANCE flags fire,
        the −5 cap is lifted and Penalty_Total can go lower:
          - identity_unverified
          - individual_hours_below_requirement
          - report_mostly_blank
          - same_evidence_reused_across_reports
          - evidence_contradicts_claim
          - fabricated_document
          - stock_or_web_image (when used to falsely substantiate claims)
        In these cases, Badge Readiness is automatically set to
        Resubmission Required.

Track_A_Red_Flags         = sum of red-flag penalties from 5.1/5.2
                            (Medium: −1 to −2 each;
                             High:   −2 to −3 each;
                             Critical: up to −5 each)

Track_B_Section_Inconsistencies = sum of all section-level penalty
                                  triggers from 5.3, with each section's
                                  contribution clamped at the section cap.

The global cap of −5 means: even if a report has many inconsistencies
across multiple sections, the CII penalty caps at −5. This protects
honest students from being crushed by accumulated small flaws while
still meaningfully reducing the score for genuinely problematic work.
```

### 5.5 Penalty Reporting Requirements

Every penalty applied must be:

1. **Named** by its exact trigger ID (e.g., `target_drift`, not "SDG issue").
2. **Quantified** with its specific value (e.g., −1.0).
3. **Located** with the section/component where it triggered.
4. **Explained** in one sentence the student can understand.

The Final Score Table (Output Component B) must show:
- Track A subtotal
- Track B subtotal
- Whether the −5 global cap was binding
- Whether any critical non-compliance flag was raised

Example penalty report row:

```
Section 4: triggered type_category_mismatch (−1.0)
  → "Output type 'Sessions Conducted' was paired with the activity
     category 'Resource Distribution', which is incompatible. Please
     select a matching output type next time."
```

## PART 6 — BADGE READINESS DECISION TREE

Badge Readiness is **separate from CII**. Assign one of:

### 6.1 Ready for Badge
Use when ALL of:
- CII is computed,
- identity is verified,
- individual hours meet requirement,
- evidence supports major claims,
- no serious red flags.

### 6.2 Conditional Badge
Use when:
- work is meaningful,
- CII is valid,
- but 1–2 evidence issues should be corrected before issuance.

### 6.3 Admin Review Required
Use when ANY of:
- contradictions exist between sections,
- individual evidence is unclear,
- partner role is unclear,
- unrealistic hours appear,
- student-level proof is missing,
- moderate integrity concerns are present.

### 6.4 Resubmission Required
Use when ANY of:
- identity is missing,
- individual hours not met,
- core sections are blank,
- evidence is absent,
- serious HEC compliance failure exists.

> A student may score Level 3 or Level 4 on CII but still require Admin Review.


## PART 7 — PER-SECTION RUBRICS

Each section follows a unified format:
1. **Weight & Floor**
2. **Components / Form Fields evaluated**
3. **Scoring rubric (anchors + bands)**

## SECTION 1 — IDENTITY & PARTICIPATION

**Weight:** 10 points | **Recognition Floor:** 7.0 | **Critical Rule:** Individual hours matter — team aggregate hours never replace individual verified hours.

### 1.A Components Evaluated
- Student name, student ID, email verification, mobile verification, CNIC or identity verification.
- Institution, degree program, department, year of study.
- Participation type, role in team.
- **Individual verified hours, required hours of service, attendance sessions, active days, attendance realism.**
- Participation approval, HEC compliance.

### 1.B Critical Compliance Formula
```
Required_Hours = max(16, opportunity_required_hours)
Compliance_Ratio = Logged_Hours / Required_Hours

If Compliance_Ratio < 1.0 → report cannot be finalized;
   Section 1 capped at 6.0/10 with badge_readiness = Resubmission Required.
```

### 1.C Anchor Guidance

| Anchor | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **5 (9.5)** | Identity verified; individual hours exceed requirement; sustained participation across multiple days; clear role; realistic attendance; strong documentation. |
| **4 (8.2)** | Identity verified; required hours met; role clear; reasonable attendance; participation approved. |
| **3 (6.8)** | Identity verified and required hours met; attendance detail or role clarity basic. |
| **2 (5.5 → 7.0 floor)** | Hours appear met but documentation thin or compressed. |
| **1 (4.0 → 7.0 floor)** | Very limited participation detail, but some verification exists. |
| **0** | Identity not verified or individual hours clearly not met. |

### 1.F Commentary Must Include
- Whether identity is verified.
- Whether individual hours meet the requirement.
- Whether participation is individual-level or team-level.
- Whether attendance pattern is realistic.
- What needs correction if score is low.

## SECTION 2 — PROJECT CONTEXT & DISCIPLINE

**Weight:** 10 points | **Recognition Floor:** 5.5 | **Component Allocation:** 2.2 (4.0), 2.3 (3.5), 2.4 (2.5)

### 2.A Component Breakdown

| # | Component | Status | Points |
|---|-----------|--------|--------|
| 2.1 | Project Identity | Auto-filled, read-only | 0.0 (validation only) |
| 2.2 | Problem / System Need | Mandatory — 100–200 words | **4.0** |
| 2.3 | Academic Discipline Applied | Mandatory — dropdown + 100–200 words | **3.5** |
| 2.4 | Baseline Evidence Source | Required — multi-select + summary | **2.5** |

### 2.B Component 2.2 — Problem / System Need (4.0 pts)

Score elements **A–D** (each 25% of internal score):
- **A. Specific issue or challenge** — concrete, observable conditions (e.g., "broken desks, no functional lighting in 6 of 12 classrooms"). Fail: generic complaints, abstract problems with no detail.
- **B. Affected population** — names with numbers, demographics, or named groups (e.g., "240 students, grades 3–8, SOS Village School Lahore"). Fail: unnamed beneficiaries; vague "the community".
- **C. Nature of the gap** — specifies the type (skills / access / systems / resources / infrastructure / awareness) AND explains it. Fail: names a gap category without explanation.
- **D. Necessity of structured intervention** — justifies why an organized response was needed: scale, prior failures, institutional readiness, urgency, complexity. Fail: tautological reasoning.

**Hard prohibitions (deduction triggers):**
- Describing project activities ("We installed…", "We trained…") → −20 internal.
- Describing results or outcomes ("Students improved…", "Attendance rose…") → −20 internal.
- Pure adjectives without evidence ("terrible," "shocking," "amazing need").

**Scoring rubric (internal → points of 4.0):**

| Internal | Points | Description |
|----------|--------|-------------|
| 90–100 | 3.6 – 4.0 | All four elements (A–D) fully addressed with concrete specifics. Strictly baseline-focused. |
| 75–89 | 3.0 – 3.5 | All four elements present; one element less specific but still substantive. |
| 60–74 | 2.4 – 2.9 | Three elements present, OR all four present but one is vague/generic. |
| 40–59 | 1.6 – 2.3 | Two elements present, OR significant vagueness across multiple elements. |
| 0–39 | 0.0 – 1.5 | Fewer than two elements addressed; off-topic; substantially describes activities/results. |

### 2.C Component 2.3 — Academic Discipline Applied (3.5 pts)

**2.3a Discipline Selection (0.35 pts):** Binary — 0.35 if a valid selection from the dropdown is present, 0.00 otherwise. If empty → entire Component 2.3 scores 0 and is flagged.

**2.3b Problem Statement Summary (3.15 pts):** 100–200 words explaining how the discipline informed the analysis.

Elements:
- **A. Named discipline-specific tools/frameworks** — e.g., "stakeholder mapping," "thermal load analysis," "participatory action research." Fail: no named tools.
- **B. Connection to problem analysis** — explains HOW the tools were used to understand the baseline.
- **C. Design contribution** — how disciplinary knowledge shaped the structured approach (not outcomes).
- **D. Alignment with selected discipline** — tools named plausibly drawn from the discipline chosen in 2.3a. Misalignment → **discipline_mismatch** flag.

### 2.D Component 2.4 — Baseline Evidence Source (2.5 pts)

**2.4a Evidence Selection (1.5 pts):**
- Element A: At least one selection made (mandatory; non-empty).
- Element B: Plausibility — sources reasonable given project context.
- Element C: Sufficiency — combination adequate to substantiate claims in 2.2.

| Internal | Points (of 1.5) | Description |
|----------|-----------------|-------------|
| 90–100 | 1.35 – 1.50 | Multiple complementary sources (quantitative + qualitative); strong alignment with 2.2. |
| 75–89 | 1.13 – 1.34 | Two or more sources selected; aligned with 2.2. |
| 60–74 | 0.90 – 1.11 | One substantive source; aligned with 2.2. |
| 40–59 | 0.60 – 0.89 | Source selected but weak alignment with 2.2 claims. |
| 0–39 | 0.00 – 0.59 | No selection, OR sources implausible / contradictory with 2.2. |

**2.4b System-Generated Summary (1.0 pts):** Scored on coherence and alignment with 2.2 and 2.3. Flag `summary_contradiction` if inconsistent.

### 2.E Section 2 Aggregate

```
Score_Section_2 = Score_2.2 + Score_2.3 + Score_2.4
Maximum: 10.0       Minimum: 0.0       Round to 2 decimals.
```

### 2.F Section-Level Flags
`data_integrity`, `prohibited_content`, `discipline_mismatch`, `evidence_problem_misalignment`, `summary_contradiction`.

### 2.G Student-Facing Example
> *"The project purpose is clear and relevant. To move higher, add more local context — the specific community need, beneficiary profile, or problem observed before starting the activity."*

## SECTION 3 — SDG STRATEGY & INTENT

**Weight:** 10 points | **Recognition Floor:** 5.5 | **Defining feature:** Dynamic-weighted optional slots.

### 3.A Component Breakdown

| # | Component | Status | Default Points | Behaviour |
|---|-----------|--------|----------------|-----------|
| 3.1 | Opportunity's Registered SDGs | Locked, display-only | 0.0 | Validation only |
| 3.1.1 | Primary Contribution Logic Statement | Required — ≤200 words | **7.0** | Base — absorbs unused slot points |
| 3.2 | Additional SDG Slot #1 | Optional | **1.5** | Redistributable |
| 3.2.2 | Additional SDG Slot #2 | Optional | **1.5** | Redistributable |

### 3.B Dynamic Weighting Rule
- **UNUSED slot** → 1.5 points roll up to 3.1.1's maximum.
- **USED & VALID** → up to 1.5 points earned.
- **USED but poorly aligned (FAKE)** → points LOST + **−0.5 section deduction**.
- **USED — PARTIAL** (SDG selected, no Target OR no logic) → slot capped at 50% of max.

**Effective_Max for 3.1.1:**
- Both slots UNUSED → 10.0
- One UNUSED, one USED → 8.5
- Both USED → 7.0

### 3.C Component 3.1.1 — Primary Contribution Logic Statement

Elements:
- **A. Pathway clarity** — explains causal chain: activity → mechanism → SDG target outcome.
- **B. Beneficiary specification** — names who benefits with scale, demographics, or context.
- **C. Specific shift articulated** — concrete observable change, tied to chosen Target.
- **D. Target / Indicator alignment** — logic maps to the chosen SDG Target's intent; no drift to a different SDG.

**Hard prohibitions:**
- Listing activities without articulating the causal chain.
- Generic SDG language with no Target-specific mapping.
- Drifting to a different SDG's outcomes (target_drift flag).
- Vague impact claims with no observable shift named.

**Score:** internal 0–100 → `Score_3.1.1 = (internal/100) × Effective_Max_3.1.1`

### 3.D Component 3.2 / 3.2.2 — Additional SDG Slots

Each slot: Selection (0.5 pts) + Logic (1.0 pts).

| Selection State | Selection Points |
|-----------------|------------------|
| SDG + Target + Indicator selected, alignment genuine | 0.50 |
| SDG + Target selected, no Indicator, alignment genuine | 0.40 |
| SDG selected only (no Target) | 0.20 (PARTIAL cap) |
| Empty | UNUSED — redistributes |
| Selection made but FAKE | 0.00 + section −0.5 |

**Cross-slot rule:** Slot #2 selecting the same SDG as Slot #1 or a locked SDG → automatically FAKE.

### 3.E Section 3 Aggregate

```
Score_Section_3 = Score_3.1.1 + Score_3.2 + Score_3.2.2 − Section_Deductions
Clamp to [0.0, 10.0], round to 2 decimals.

Where Effective_Max_3.1.1 is dynamic (7.0 / 8.5 / 10.0).
Section_Deductions: −0.5 per FAKE slot.
```

### 3.F Section-Level Flags
`locked_sdg_invalid`, `target_drift`, `forced_alignment`, `duplicate_sdg`, `activities_only`, `scope_mismatch`.

### 3.G Student-Facing Example
> *"Your SDG selection is relevant. The score can improve if you explain the exact SDG target and show how your activity moves from action to output to outcome."*

## SECTION 4 — ACTIVITIES & OUTPUT SCALE

**Weight:** 15 points | **Recognition Floor:** 8.0 | **Defining feature:** 70/30 Quality–Quantity split.

### 4.A The 70 / 30 Quality–Quantity Split

| Dimension | Points | Captures |
|-----------|--------|----------|
| **Quality (Q)** | **10.5 (70%)** | Specificity of description, fit between activity and category, verifiability of outputs, depth of beneficiary categorisation, rigor of verification language, clarity of geography. |
| **Quantity / Reach (N)** | **4.5 (30%)** | Mode-adjusted, log-scaled beneficiary count. Online-mass-reach heavily discounted; field-based depth rewarded. |

### 4.B Per-Activity Components

| # | Component | Dimension | Points |
|---|-----------|-----------|--------|
| 4.1 | Activity Blocks (title, status, category, description 50–100 words) | Quality | 3.0 |
| 4.2 | Delivery Execution (mode, model, sessions, explanation) | Quality | 2.5 |
| 4.3 | Measurable Outputs (title, type, qty, unit, verification) | Quality | 2.5 |
| 4.4 | Beneficiary Reach | Quality + Quantity | 2.5 Q + 4.5 N |
| 4.5 | Geographic Reach | folded into 4.4Q | — |
| 4.6 | Project-Level Summary | Coherence multiplier | 0.80 – 1.00 |
| 4.7 | Implementation Scale Summary | folded into 4.6 | — |

### 4.C Quantity Calculation (4.4 N)

**Step 1 — Raw band from beneficiary count:**

| Beneficiary count | Pre-mode raw score |
|-------------------|--------------------|
| 0 | 0 |
| 1–9 | 30 |
| 10–49 | 55 |
| 50–199 | 75 |
| 200–999 | 88 |
| 1,000–9,999 | 95 |
| 10,000+ | 100 (ceiling) |

**Step 2 — Mode-engagement factor matrix:**

| Implementation Model / Mode | Field | Hybrid | Online |
|-----------------------------|-------|--------|--------|
| Individual | 0.90 | 0.70 | 0.30 |
| Team-Based | 1.00 | 0.80 | 0.35 |
| Partner-Led | 0.95 | 0.75 | 0.30 |
| Multi-Stakeholder | 1.00 | 0.85 | 0.40 |
| Volunteer-Supported | 0.85 | 0.65 | 0.25 |
| Institutionally Coordinated | 1.00 | 0.80 | 0.35 |
| Community-Led | 1.00 | 0.85 | 0.40 |

**Step 3 — Overlap multiplier:**

| Overlap status | Multiplier |
|----------------|------------|
| Mostly Unique to This Activity | 1.00 |
| Partially Overlapping | 0.85 |
| Mostly the Same Beneficiaries | 0.50 |
| Not Known | 0.70 (honesty penalty) |

**Final per-activity N:**
```
Score_4.4N (per activity) = (raw_band/100) × mode_factor × overlap_mult × 4.5
If number_inflation: cap at 50% of computed value.
```

### 4.D Section 4 Aggregate

```
For each activity i:
  Q_i = Score_4.1 + Score_4.2 + Score_4.3 + Score_4.4Q   (max 10.5)
  N_i = Score_4.4N   (max 4.5)
  T_i = Q_i + N_i    (max 15.0)
  If T_i < 4.0: raise padding_low_quality_activity

mean_T = sum(T_i) / n
breadth_factor = min(1.0 + 0.05 × (n-1), 1.10)   # cap at n=3
coherence_multiplier from 4.6 / 4.7 (0.80 – 1.00)

Section_4_score = mean_T × breadth_factor × coherence_multiplier
Clamp to [0.0, 15.0].
```

### 4.E Output Type × Activity Category Compatibility Table
Mismatch triggers `type_category_mismatch` flag and **−20 internal** on that output. See full mapping in Appendix A.

### 4.F Section-Level Flags
`category_other_overuse`, `type_category_mismatch`, `verification_weak`, `number_inflation`, `scale_overclaim`, `mode_mismatch`, `padding_low_quality_activity`, `project_count_math_error`.

### 4.G Student-Facing Example
> *"Your project reached a smaller group, but the engagement appears meaningful because the support was focused and directly connected to beneficiary learning. To move higher, add attendance sheets, activity photos, or beneficiary feedback to confirm the depth of work."*

## SECTION 5 — OUTCOMES & MEASURABLE CHANGE

**Weight:** 10 points | **Recognition Floor:** 5.5 | **Defining feature:** 70/30 split + confidence-adjusted outcome scoring.

### 5.A Components

| # | Component | Scope | Dimension | Points |
|---|-----------|-------|-----------|--------|
| 5.1 | Observed Change Narrative (100–200 words) | Project-level | Quality | 2.5 |
| 5.2 | Measurable Outcomes (per outcome) | Per outcome | Quality + Quantity | 3.0 Q + 3.0 N per outcome (mean) |
| 5.3 | Challenges & Limitations (50–200 words) | Project-level | Quality (honesty) | 1.5 |
| — | Section 4 Coherence Check | Cross-section | Multiplier | × 0.80 – 1.00 |

### 5.B Critical Rule: Output ≠ Outcome
- **Output:** "50 students attended a session."
- **Outcome:** "35 students improved their understanding from baseline to endline."

### 5.C Component 5.1 — Observed Change Narrative

Elements:
- **A. Before (Baseline)** — pre-project state stated specifically.
- **B. After (Change)** — post-project state with comparable measure.
- **C. Link to Section 4 activities** — names which activity produced the change.
- **D. What beneficiaries can now do differently** — concrete behavioural change.

### 5.D Component 5.2 — Measurable Outcomes (per outcome)

**Quality (3.0 pts/outcome):**
- A. Outcome Category fit (16 options)
- B. Metric Group compatibility (12 options)
- C. Baseline + Endline meaningful
- D. Confidence Level honestly chosen

**Quantity (3.0 pts/outcome) — three stages:**

**Stage 1 — Improvement % bands (Case A: baseline > 0):**

| Improvement % | Pre-confidence raw |
|---------------|--------------------|
| ≤ 0% | 0 |
| 1–10% | 30 |
| 11–25% | 50 |
| 26–50% | 70 |
| 51–100% | 85 |
| 101–200% | 92 |
| 201–500% | 97 |
| 500%+ | 100 (ceiling) |

**Stage 1 alt — Baseline-zero caps (Case B: baseline = 0, endline > 0):**

| Confidence | Baseline-zero raw cap |
|------------|----------------------|
| Directly Measured | 75 |
| Partner Confirmed | 70 |
| Observed | 55 |
| Estimated | 40 |

**Stage 2 — Confidence multiplier:**

| Confidence | Multiplier |
|------------|------------|
| Directly Measured | 1.00 |
| Partner Confirmed | 0.85 |
| Observed | 0.65 |
| Estimated | 0.40 |

**Stage 3 — Final formula:**
```
Score_5.2N (per outcome) = (raw_band/100) × confidence_mult × 3.0

If number_inflation_unverified (500%+ with Observed/Estimated): × 0.5
If confidence_overclaim (DM claimed but Section 4 verification weak):
   confidence_mult forced to 0.65 (Observed level)
```

### 5.E Section 4 Coherence Multiplier

| Coherence Result | Multiplier |
|------------------|------------|
| Full — outcomes trace cleanly to S4 activities; confidence aligns with verification | 1.00 |
| Minor — one weak link | 0.95 |
| Notable — multiple weak links; "DM" used liberally | 0.90 |
| Error — 5.1 references activities not in S4; or reach > S4 total | 0.80 + coherence_failure flag |

### 5.F Section 5 Aggregate

```
mean_T = sum(T_outcomes) / n
breadth_factor = min(1.0 + 0.05 × (n-1), 1.10)
weighted_5.2 = min(mean_T × breadth_factor, 6.0)

Section_5_raw = Score_5.1 + weighted_5.2 + Score_5.3
Section_5_score = Section_5_raw × coherence_multiplier
Clamp to [0.0, 10.0].
```

### 5.G Section-Level Flags
`orphan_outcome`, `category_metric_mismatch`, `confidence_overclaim`, `number_inflation_unverified`, `baseline_endline_regression`, `no_challenges_claim`, `coherence_failure`, `duplicate_outcome`, `padding_low_quality_outcome`.

### 5.H Student-Facing Example
> *"The report shows that beneficiaries were engaged and some learning change is described. The score is limited because the outcome is mostly narrative. A short pre/post form, partner confirmation, or beneficiary feedback would raise this section."*

## SECTION 6 — RESOURCE MOBILIZATION

**Weight:** 15 points base + up to **+3 section-internal bonus** = **max 18 points** | **Recognition Floor:** 8.0 | **Principle:** This is the **most generously-scored section** in the CIEL rubric. CIEL PK believes that *any* form of resource a student mobilizes — financial, in-kind, skill-based, digital, creative, human, time — deserves substantial appreciation. Evidence strengthens the score; absence of evidence does **not** crush it.

> **Section-specific design:** Section 6 is the only section where students can earn points BEYOND the section's face weight via an **internal bonus pool of up to +3**. This is in addition to the Master-Level Resource Mobilization Bonus (+1.5) in Part 4. A student who mobilizes meaningful resources across multiple categories can earn up to **18/15 base + 1.5 master = 19.5 total contribution to CII** from Section 6.

### 6.A Form-Field Mapping

| Field | What It Captures |
|-------|------------------|
| 6.2.1 | Resource Type (16 categories: Financial, In-kind, Equipment, Infrastructure, Digital, Human Resources, Transport, Energy, Communication/Media, Policy/Legal, Research/Data, Community Mobilization, Corporate/CSR, Government, International Dev, Other) |
| 6.2.2 / 6.2.3 | Amount + Unit (PKR, USD, Number, Hours, Units, Kg, Liters, Sessions, Licenses, Devices, Kits, Other) |
| 6.2.4 | Source (Students, Partner Org, University, Government, Private Sponsor, Corporate/CSR, Community, International, Self-Funded, Other) |
| 6.2.5 | Purpose (50–200 words) |
| 6.2.6 | Verification Status (Evidence Uploaded, Partner Confirmed, University Confirmed, Official Documentation, Self-Reported, Pending) |
| 6.3 | Optional Evidence Upload |
| "+ Add Resource Entry" | Multiple entries supported |

### 6.B Base Rubric (15 pts) — 5 Criteria, each worth up to 3.0 pts

Each criterion is scored on a **6-step half-point scale: 0.0 / 0.5 / 1.0 / 1.5 / 2.0 / 2.5 / 3.0**. This provides mathematical precision and rewards even partial fulfilment.

#### Criterion A — Resource Type Identification & Diversity (3.0 pts)

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **3.0** | 3+ distinct resource types identified across categories (e.g., Financial + In-kind + Human Resources, or Equipment + Digital + Transport) |
| **2.5** | 2+ distinct types, each clearly categorized |
| **2.0** | 2 distinct types identified |
| **1.5** | 1 type identified with detail |
| **1.0** | 1 type identified, basic |
| **0.5** | Resource mentioned but type unclear |
| **0.0** | No resource information |

#### Criterion B — Quantification & Specificity (3.0 pts)

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **3.0** | All entries fully quantified with specific amount + appropriate unit (e.g., "₨50,000", "100 books", "40 volunteer hours", "20 hygiene kits") |
| **2.5** | All entries quantified; minor unit ambiguity in one |
| **2.0** | Most entries quantified with amount + unit |
| **1.5** | Some entries quantified; others approximate |
| **1.0** | Resources mentioned with approximate scope |
| **0.5** | Vague magnitudes only ("some money", "a few items") |
| **0.0** | No quantification |

#### Criterion C — Source Documentation (3.0 pts)

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **3.0** | 3+ distinct sources cleanly named across resource entries (e.g., Student + Corporate Sponsor + Community + Partner Org) |
| **2.5** | 2–3 sources clearly named |
| **2.0** | 2 distinct sources named |
| **1.5** | 1 source named with detail |
| **1.0** | 1 source named, basic |
| **0.5** | Source category ticked but unclear |
| **0.0** | No source information |

#### Criterion D — Purpose & Project Linkage (3.0 pts) — 50–200 words per entry

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **3.0** | Each resource explicitly linked to specific Section 4 activity AND specific Section 5 outcome; purpose text 100–200 words, specific |
| **2.5** | Most resources linked to specific activities; purpose 100+ words |
| **2.0** | Resources linked to activities at general level; purpose 50–100 words |
| **1.5** | Some linkage shown; purpose written but generic |
| **1.0** | Linkage implied but not stated; purpose minimal |
| **0.5** | Purpose blank or under 50 words |
| **0.0** | No purpose information |

#### Criterion E — Verification & Effort Documentation (3.0 pts)

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **3.0** | Strong verification (receipts, partner letters, signed acknowledgments, photos with dates, official invoices) |
| **2.5** | Solid verification (receipts OR partner letters OR signed records) |
| **2.0** | Some verification (photos, WhatsApp/email communication trail) |
| **1.5** | Self-reported but narrative is detailed and credible — *floor for honest reporting* |
| **1.0** | Self-reported, basic detail |
| **0.5** | Self-reported, minimal detail |
| **0.0** | No information on how resources flowed (only when text is blank or contradictory) |

> **EVIDENCE-LENIENT RULE (mandatory):** If evidence is absent but the contribution narrative is **specific** (names amounts, types, sources, purposes) AND **credible** (consistent with the student's role, project scope, and other sections), Criterion E receives **at least 1.5**. The absence of receipts/photos does NOT push Criterion E below 1.5 when the rest of the report is solid. This is a deliberate CIEL PK policy — students who genuinely mobilized resources but didn't keep paperwork are still recognized.

### 6.C Base Score Formula

```
Base_Score_Section_6 = Criterion_A + Criterion_B + Criterion_C + Criterion_D + Criterion_E
                     (each 0.0 – 3.0, half-point steps)
Maximum Base_Score = 15.0
Apply Recognition Floor 8.0 if any substantive content exists.
```

### 6.D Section-Internal Bonus Pool (up to +3.0)

This is a Section 6-specific bonus pool — separate from and additive to the Master-Level Bonus 1 (Resource Mobilization, +1.5). Students who go above and beyond in mobilizing resources earn extra points HERE first, before any master-level bonus is considered.

| Bonus # | Trigger | Max |
|---------|---------|-----|
| **6.B1 — Multi-Category Breadth** | 4+ distinct resource categories mobilized across the project (across all entries combined) | **+0.5** |
| **6.B2 — Multi-Source Coordination** | 3+ distinct sources successfully tapped (e.g., Personal + Sponsor + Partner + Community) | **+0.5** |
| **6.B3 — High-Value Personal Contribution** | Student personally contributed ANY ONE of: ≥40 hours of skilled work (teaching/coordination/design/technical); OR personally raised ≥₨25,000 from sponsors; OR designed/built a meaningful asset (toolkit, curriculum, website, training materials) | **+1.0** |
| **6.B4 — In-Kind Resource Excellence** | Mobilized in-kind goods (books, equipment, food, clothing, hygiene kits, teaching materials) with declared value ≥₨50,000 equivalent | **+0.5** |
| **6.B5 — Digital / Creative Resources** | Created and deployed digital or creative assets for the project: social media campaigns, photography, video editing, website/app, training materials, design work | **+0.5** |
| **6.B6 — Sustained Commitment** | Resource contribution sustained across the entire project timeline (not one-time) — evidenced by multiple dated entries or ongoing engagement | **+0.5** |
| **6.B7 — Sponsor / External Mobilization** | Successfully secured external sponsor support (corporate, foundation, government, international) — even modest amounts count | **+0.5** |
| | **TOTAL CAP** | **+3.0** |

**Bonus pool capped at +3.0** even if more bonuses apply. List all earned bonuses transparently in evaluator notes; cap the sum.

### 6.E Section 6 Final Score Formula

```
Section_6_Score = clamp( Base_Score + Section_Internal_Bonus,  0,  18 )

Where:
  Base_Score                = Σ Criteria A–E (max 15.0)
  Section_Internal_Bonus    = Σ Bonuses 6.B1–6.B7 (max 3.0)
  Apply Recognition Floor 8.0 if any substantive content (replaces Base_Score
    if Base_Score < 8.0).

Section 6 may exceed its nominal 15-point weight by up to +3 via
the internal bonus pool. This is the ONLY section permitted to do so.

The Master-Level Resource Mobilization Bonus (Part 4, +1.5) is evaluated **individually** after section scoring. It is added to `Obtained_Score` with other Part 4 bonuses (not inside Section 6's line score). Final CII is always clamped to 100.
```

### 6.F Worked Examples

**Example 1 — Generous credit for time-rich, evidence-light contribution:**
- Student contributed 60 hours of teaching (Human Resources)
- Designed a 40-page workbook for students (Digital/Creative)
- Coordinated 8 volunteer days (Community Mobilization)
- No receipts; only photos of teaching and a draft workbook PDF

Scoring:
- Criterion A: 3.0 (3 distinct types)
- Criterion B: 2.5 (all quantified: 60h, 40 pages, 8 days)
- Criterion C: 2.0 (Student + Community sources)
- Criterion D: 2.5 (linked to S4 activities)
- Criterion E: 1.5 (evidence-lenient floor for honest reporting)
- **Base = 11.5 / 15**

Bonuses:

## SECTION 8 — EVIDENCE & VERIFICATION

**Weight:** 10 points | **Recognition Floor:** 5.5 | **Defining role:** This is where claims meet proof. AI does cross-section verification here.

### 8.A Form-Field Mapping
- **8.1** "Do you have evidence?" (Yes / No)
- **8.2** Evidence Type categories (8 multi-select): Activity photos, Attendance sheet, Training materials, Partner letter/email, Survey results, Media coverage, Resource delivery proof, Other
- **8.3** Evidence Description (100–200 words mandatory)
- **8.4** Ethical Declaration (4 boxes, all required)
- **8.5** Media Visibility Preference (Public / Limited / Internal)
- **8.6** Partner Verification (Yes / No)

### 8.B Anchor Guidance

| Anchor | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **5 (9.5)** | Strong evidence across hours, activities, outputs, resources, outcomes, partner role. |
| **4 (8.2)** | Evidence supports most major claims. |
| **3 (6.8)** | Some evidence supports activities but not all claims. |
| **2 (5.5)** | Thin evidence exists but leaves gaps. |
| **1 (4.0 → 5.5 floor)** | Evidence missing or weak, but report has some self-reported detail. |
| **0** | No evidence and no verification. |

### 8.C Evidence Tiers (Quality of Evidence)
- **Weak:** Self-reported only.
- **Basic:** Photos or screenshots without context.
- **Moderate:** Photos + attendance, receipts, or communication trail.
- **Strong:** Partner confirmation, supervisor confirmation, dated records, receipts.
- **Excellent:** Partner letter, attendance, receipts, photos/videos, beneficiary feedback.
- **Transformative:** Independent verification, measurable outcome proof, continuation proof.

### 8.D The Cross-Section Claim-to-Evidence Matrix (Core Mechanism)

The AI builds an explicit matrix matching each claim from prior sections to evidence files. For each claim:
- `matched_file_ids` — files supporting it
- `match_confidence` — 0–100
- `match_status` — VERIFIED ≥75 / PARTIAL 50–74 / WEAK 25–49 / UNVERIFIED <25
- `mismatch_notes` — specific gaps

**Coverage rate drives section anchor:**
- ≥75% claims verified → strong anchor (4–5)
- 40–74% → moderate (3)
- <40% → weak (1–2)

**Matching examples:**
- "Served food to children" → file with `depicted_activity = serving_food` AND `depicted_subjects` including children.
- "Trained students in classroom" → `depicted_setting = classroom` AND `depicted_activity = teaching/training`.
- "₨20,000 spent on kits" → receipt with extracted amount near 20,000 OR delivery photo with kits visible.

### 8.E Ethical Compliance Image Check
- Minors visible AND faces not blurred AND no consent form → `minor_consent_gap` (High flag, especially with Public visibility).
- Dignity concerns / exploitation framing → `dignity_concern`.
- These cap Section 8 Criterion D regardless of ethical box ticks.

### 8.F Lenient Rule
Do not erase real work because evidence is weak. Instead, reduce **Evidence Confidence** and **Badge Readiness**. Section can still score moderately if the work was real.

### 8.G Hard Gates
- "No — I do not have evidence" → Section 8 capped at 6/10.
- Any of {`internal_duplicate`, `stock_or_web_image`, `fabricated_document`} → capped at 4/10 + manual review required.

### 8.H Section-Level Flags
`internal_duplicate`, `stock_or_web_image`, `fabricated_document`, `minor_consent_gap`, `identity_mismatch`, `geographic_impossibility`, `dignity_concern`.

### 8.I Student-Facing Example
> *"The evidence shows that some activity took place, but it does not yet fully verify the beneficiary count or your individual contribution. Uploading student-level attendance, partner confirmation, and activity photos would raise both confidence and badge readiness."*

## SECTION 9 — PERSONAL & ACADEMIC REFLECTION

**Weight:** 5 points | **Recognition Floor:** 2.7 | **Scoring philosophy:** Authentic reflection, not polished writing.

### 9.A Components

| # | Component | Status | Points |
|---|-----------|--------|--------|
| 9.0 | Academic Integration Level | Mandatory single-select | 0.5 |
| 9.1 | Personal Learning Reflection (100–200 words) | Mandatory | 1.75 |
| 9.2 | Academic Application & Discipline Contribution (100–200 words) | Mandatory | 1.75 |
| 9.3 | Competency Self-Assessment (12 ratings, 1–5 scale) | Mandatory | 1.0 |

### 9.B Reflection vs Description (The Headline Anti-Pattern)
If a paragraph could fit unchanged into Section 4 (Activities), it does NOT count as reflection. **Description ≠ Reflection.**

| Description (does NOT count) | Reflection (counts) |
|------------------------------|---------------------|
| "We organised three classroom-painting sessions." | "I had assumed turnout would track enthusiasm, but it tracked transport. That changed how I think about access in volunteer programs." |
| "I learned about teamwork." | "When two team members disagreed on the survey wording, I realised I default to consensus and miss precision." |

### 9.C Component 9.0 — Academic Integration Level (0.5 pts)
- 0.25 for valid selection.
- 0.25 for consistency with 9.2 depth claim.

Integration levels set depth expectations for 9.2:
- **Voluntary Extracurricular** — baseline depth.
- **Course-Linked Assignment** — moderate depth; specific course concepts named.
- **Credit-Bearing Component** — strong depth; formal frameworks applied.
- **Capstone / Thesis** — high depth; independent application of advanced concepts.
- **Research-Integrated** — highest depth; methodology, data analysis visible.

### 9.D Component 9.1 — Personal Learning (1.75 pts)
Four prompts: A. New skills | B. Community insights | C. Perspective changes | D. Challenges (mandatory).

Score 0–100 internal → × 1.75.
- `description_as_reflection` flag if ≥60% of text is activity recap → −20 internal.

### 9.E Component 9.2 — Academic Application (1.75 pts)
Four elements: A. Field of study named | B. Specific concepts/methods applied | C. Connection to actual project work | D. Depth appropriate to 9.0.

- `integration_depth_mismatch` if 9.0 claims high depth but 9.2 shows voluntary-level depth → −15 internal.

### 9.F Component 9.3 — Competency Self-Assessment (1.0 pts)
**Scoring philosophy: pattern, not rating value.** A "5" is not better than a "3" — what matters is whether ratings are complete, authentic, and consistent with 9.1/9.2.

| Sub-component | Points | Rule |
|---------------|--------|------|
| Completeness | 0.40 | (# rated / 12) × 0.40 |
| Authenticity | 0.30 | Starts at 0.30; decreases with suspicious patterns |
| Cross-consistency | 0.30 | Sample 2–3 high-rated and 1–2 low-rated; check narrative support |

Authenticity penalties:
- All 5s → `self_inflation` (−0.20)
- All 1s → `disengagement_pattern` (−0.20)
- All identical → `uniform_pattern` (−0.15)
- Mean ≥4.5 with no advanced evidence → `unsubstantiated_inflation` (−0.15)

### 9.G Section 9 Aggregate

```
Score_Section_9 = Score_9.0 + Score_9.1 + Score_9.2 + Score_9.3
Clamp to [0.0, 5.0].
```

### 9.H Section-Level Flags
`description_as_reflection`, `integration_depth_mismatch`, `self_inflation`, `disengagement_pattern`, `uniform_pattern`, `unsubstantiated_inflation`, `competency_evidence_gap`.

### 9.I Student-Facing Example
> *"Your reflection shows awareness of what you experienced during the project. To improve, connect your learning more directly to one specific challenge, one academic concept, and one change in how you view community service."*

## PART 8 — CROSS-SECTION COHERENCE MAP

The CIEL evaluation system treats the report as a single connected document, not 10 independent silos. Coherence ties must be checked because students' claims in one section should be supported elsewhere.

### 8.1 Critical Coherence Ties

| From → To | Check |
|-----------|-------|
| **S1 (Attendance) → S4 (Activities)** | Logged session dates and locations align with Activity Block dates and locations. |
| **S2 (Discipline 2.3) → S9 (Application 9.2)** | The discipline named in S2.3a must match the discipline framing in S9.2. Mismatch → `discipline_mismatch`. |
| **S3 (Locked SDG) → S4 (Activities)** | Activities should plausibly contribute to the locked SDG target. |
| **S3 (Slot Mapping) → S4 (Activities)** | Additional SDG slot must connect to a real activity. Forced alignment → FAKE → −0.5. |
| **S4 (Verification language) → S5 (Confidence)** | If S4 says "verified by team" with no instrument, S5 cannot claim "Directly Measured" without triggering `confidence_overclaim`. |
| **S4 (Distinct total) → S5 (Reach claims)** | S5 outcomes' implied reach must not exceed S4's project-level distinct beneficiary count. |
| **S4 (Activity titles) → S5 (Narrative 5.1)** | Activities named in S5.1 narrative must exist in S4. Otherwise → `coherence_failure`. |
| **S4 (Activities) → S8 (Evidence)** | Each activity claim should have matching evidence files. |
| **S5 (Outcomes) → S8 (Evidence)** | Baseline/endline claims need attendance sheets, assessment records, or partner confirmation. |
| **S6 (Resources) → S8 (Evidence)** | Each resource claim should have receipts, partner letters, or photos in S8. |
| **S6 (Sponsor source) → S7 (Partnerships)** | Corporate/CSR resource source should appear as a Partner in S7 if it played a partner role. |
| **S7 (Partners) → S8 (Evidence)** | Each partner claim needs MOU, letter, email, or right-sized informal evidence. |
| **S7 (Partner roles) → S10 (Continuation)** | "Partner-led Continuation" requires an active S7 partner with ongoing engagement language. |
| **S9.0 (Integration) → S9.2 (Application)** | Capstone/Research-Integrated claim must be matched by Capstone-level depth in 9.2 narrative. |
| **S10 (Funding Secured) → S8 (Evidence)** | Post-project funding mechanism requires distinct funding evidence (separate from initial project funding in S6). |

### 8.2 The Section 8 Cross-Section Claim Matrix
Section 8 builds an explicit **claim-to-evidence matrix** drawing from S1, S4, S5, S6, S7. Coverage rate determines Section 8 Criterion E and Master Bonus 3:

```
total_claims      = count of all verifiable claims across S1, S4, S5, S6, S7
verified_claims   = claims with status VERIFIED (≥75 confidence)
coverage_rate     = verified_claims / total_claims

coverage_rate ≥ 0.75    → Section 8 strong (Anchor 4–5)
coverage_rate 0.40–0.74 → Section 8 moderate (Anchor 3)
coverage_rate <0.40     → Section 8 weak (Anchor 1–2)

coverage_rate ≥ 0.85   → Bonus 3 (Evidence Strength) eligible
```

### 8.3 Coherence Multipliers (within sections)

Some sections apply internal coherence multipliers:

| Section | Multiplier Source | Range |
|---------|-------------------|-------|
| Section 4 | Project-level summary (4.6/4.7) | 0.80 – 1.00 |
| Section 5 | Section 4 coherence check | 0.80 – 1.00 |

These multipliers are applied at the section level and feed the final section score. They are not separate red flags; they reduce the section's score automatically.

- Never blame, never condescend, never use the phrase "you failed".

### Appendix E — Quick Reference Decision Tree

```
1. Score each section (anchor or band-based, apply floor)
       ↓
2. Sum section scores → Base CII (0–100)
       ↓
3. Apply Lenient Calibration:
   - Honest Participation Lift (raise floor to 58 if 7/10 sections + hours + identity + evidence + activity)
   - Meaningful Work Protection (don't crush evidence-weak honest work)
       ↓
4. Apply Master-Level Bonuses (max +5)
       ↓
5. Apply Red-Flag Penalties (max −5 unless critical)
       ↓
6. Final CII = clamp( Obtained_Score + Individual_Master_Bonuses − Penalties , 0 , 100 ) → assign Badge Level
       ↓
7. Assess Badge Readiness independently (Ready / Conditional / Admin Review / Resubmission)
       ↓
8. Produce required human-readable output (Parts 9.A through 9.I)
```
